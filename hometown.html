My Hometown: Sheikhupura, Punjab

About Sheikhupura

Sheikhupura is a historic city in the Punjab province of Pakistan, located about 38 kilometers northwest of Lahore. The city was founded by the Mughal Emperor Jahangir in the early 17th century and was originally named Jahangirpura after himself, but later came to be known by its current name.

Key Features

  • Population: Approximately 3.5 million people
  • District: Part of Punjab province
  • Economy: Known for agriculture, especially rice and wheat production
  • Industry: Has several industrial estates with textile and manufacturing units

Historical Significance

Sheikhupura has a rich Mughal heritage. The city is home to several historical landmarks including:

Hiran Minar

A unique Mughal-era complex built by Emperor Jahangir in memory of his pet antelope. The site features a tall minaret, a large water tank, and a pavilion.

Sheikhupura Fort

A historical fort built during the reign of Emperor Jahangir. The fort has a unique architectural style blending Mughal and Sikh influences.

Modern Sheikhupura

Today, Sheikhupura is a growing city with modern amenities while still maintaining its historical charm. The city has good educational institutions, healthcare facilities, and transportation links to major cities like Lahore and Gujranwala.
